# No Throat Clearing

Does the introduction avoid throat-clearing openings? Sentences like "Economists have long wondered about...", "Since the dawn of time...", or "An important question in the literature is..." waste the reader's attention and signal that the author hasn't found the real opening yet.

Does the first paragraph get to the point — the topic, the tension, or the question — without generic warm-up?
