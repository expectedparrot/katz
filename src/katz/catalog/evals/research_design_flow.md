# Research Design Flow

Is the research design presented with sufficient formal structure? A well-presented empirical strategy makes the model, the identifying assumptions, and the estimating equations explicit. Consider:

- Is the model being estimated written out formally (e.g., y_it = alpha_i + gamma_t + beta x_it + epsilon_it)?
- Are the identifying assumptions stated explicitly (e.g., E[epsilon_it | x_it, alpha_i, gamma_t] = 0)?
- Is the source of identifying variation clearly named — what changes, for whom, and when?
- Are all equations that will be estimated shown in the text before results are presented?

The reader should be able to assess the credibility of the design from the equations and assumptions alone, without needing to reverse-engineer the strategy from the results tables.
