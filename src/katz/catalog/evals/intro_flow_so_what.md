# The So-What

Does the introduction articulate the economic or intellectual "so-what" — what the findings mean beyond the specific context of the study? This is the interpretive leap from results to implications.

Example: "These findings point toward an important economic intuition that is often overlooked in the popular debate about television: the cognitive effects of television exposure depend critically on the educational value of the alternative activities that it crowds out."

Does the paper make this interpretive move, or does it stop at reporting results?
