# Why It Matters

Does the introduction explain why the topic matters — for policy, theory, or practice — early on? The reader should understand the stakes before the research question is stated.

Example: "Given the important role that cognitive skills play in individual (Griliches and Mason 1972) and aggregate (Bishop 1989) labor market performance, understanding the cognitive effects of television viewing may have significant implications for public policy and household behavior."

Is the importance grounded in specifics (citations, magnitudes, policy stakes), or is it asserted generically?
