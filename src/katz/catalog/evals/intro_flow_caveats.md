# Caveats and Limitations

Does the introduction acknowledge key caveats or limitations? Stating limitations early builds credibility and helps the reader calibrate expectations.

Example: "We wish to stress three important caveats. First, our identification strategy only allows us to speak to the effects of early childhood exposure."

Are the most important limitations flagged in the introduction, or does the reader have to wait until the discussion/conclusion to learn what the paper cannot show?
