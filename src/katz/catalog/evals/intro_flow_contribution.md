# Contribution Statement

Does the introduction clearly state the paper's contribution relative to existing work? The reader should understand what is new — novel data, a new measurement approach, a new source of variation, a new theoretical result.

Example: "Our study contributes to a large literature on the cognitive effects of television, most of which identifies the effect of television using cross-sectional variation in children's viewing intensity."

Does the paper claim credit specifically (novel data, new method, new variation) or generically ("we contribute to the literature on X")?
